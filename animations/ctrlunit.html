where we are: the machine that reads the opcode

Every page so far had control lines arriving from somewhere — Branch, Jump, memToReg, memWrite. This is the somewhere. The control unit is the little circuit inside the instruction decoder that reads the opcode and funct fields and raises exactly the control lines this instruction needs. And it is not magic: it is one decoder you already built, plus three gates.

The shape to hold onto: the opcode enters a 2-to-4 decoder that turns it into a one-hot row — "this instruction is that kind" — and each row either is a control line, or gets refined by a gate or two before leaving on the right.

the decoder you already built

The two opcode bits oc₁oc₀ are the decoder's address, and its enable is tied to 1 — the control unit is always listening. Hover the decoder to see the real circuit (two inverters, four AND gates); click it to drill all the way down.

Click oc₁ and oc₀ and watch the lit row march: 00 R-type, 01 control-flow, 10 load, 11 store. One row, never two.

the funct refinement — branch or jump?

Opcode 01 is a family: BEQ, BNE, and JAL share it, and the funct field tells them apart — exactly like RISC-V's funct3. The family row fam meets the funct bits: a conditional branch has f₁=0, so Branch = fam AND (NOT f₁).

A jump has f₁=1: the second AND passes fam·f₁, and one more AND with NOT f₀ pins it to funct 10 — Jump. Set oc to 01 and flip f₁/f₀: the decision walks from Branch to Jump through these three gates.

loads and stores are single rows

Not every control line needs gates. Opcode 10 is a load, so that decoder row leaves directly as memToReg; opcode 11 leaves as memWrite. And the R-type row 00 connects to nothing at all — an ADD needs no special treatment, so its row honestly dead-ends.

why this matters

Grow this to a real machine and nothing changes shape: RV32I has a 7-bit opcode, so the decoder is 7-to-128 instead of 2-to-4, and the gate cluster per family is bigger — but a control unit is still a decoder plus combinational cleanup. When an architecture manual says "the control unit asserts MemWrite for stores," this page is the entire mechanism behind that sentence.

It also explains control's cost: every new instruction format is another row and a few more gates — which is why RISC ISAs keep formats few and regular.
